# REFERENCES AND DOMAIN POLICY

## 1. Purpose of This Policy

This document defines the standards for:

* reference usage and documentation,
* verification and hallucination handling, and
* domain scope eligibility for submissions.

The objective is to ensure that all submissions maintain **traceability, transparency, and scientific integrity**, while enabling the repository to function as a dataset for analyzing AI-generated scientific outputs.

---

## 2. Core Principles

All submissions must adhere to the following principles:

### 2.1 Transparency

All references used or generated must be explicitly documented and categorized.

### 2.2 Traceability

Each claim made in the report should be traceable to:

* a verifiable reference,
* a known standard, or
* a clearly identified AI inference.

### 2.3 Reproducibility

References provided to the AI model must be documented so that the generation process can be reproduced.

### 2.4 Honesty in Verification

If a reference cannot be verified, it must be marked accordingly. False claims of verification are not permitted.

---

## 3. Classification of References

All references must be organized into the following categories:

### 3.1 Input References (Provided to AI)

These are materials explicitly included in the prompt or provided context.

Examples:

* journal articles
* textbooks
* ASTM/ISO standards
* datasets
* technical reports

**Requirement:**

* Must be cited in APA format
* Must be clearly listed under "Input References"

---

### 3.2 AI-Generated References

These are references generated by the AI model in its output.

**Requirement:**

* Must be listed exactly as generated
* Must not be modified or corrected
* Must be clearly labeled as "AI-Generated References"

---

### 3.3 Verified References

These are references that have been checked for authenticity.

**Verification Methods May Include:**

* DOI lookup
* Google Scholar search
* Publisher website validation
* Standard database confirmation (ASTM, ISO, etc.)

**Verification Status Tags:**

* ✓ VERIFIED
* ⚠ NOT VERIFIED
* ✗ HALLUCINATION

---

## 4. Reference Formatting

All references must follow **APA 7th Edition** format.

### Example:

Smith, J., Kumar, R., & Lee, P. (2020). Fatigue behavior of aluminum alloys. *International Journal of Fatigue*, 135, 105567. [https://doi.org/xxxxx](https://doi.org/xxxxx)

---

## 5. Handling AI Hallucinations

AI hallucinations are a **key research focus** of this repository and must be documented, not hidden.

### 5.1 Types of Hallucinations

* Fabricated references (non-existent papers)
* Incorrect DOIs or publication details
* Misattributed authors or journals
* Physically impossible scientific claims

### 5.2 Required Action

* Do NOT delete hallucinated references
* Mark them clearly as ✗ HALLUCINATION
* Provide brief explanation if possible

### 5.3 Importance

Documenting hallucinations contributes to:

* AI reliability research
* benchmarking model performance
* understanding failure modes in scientific reasoning

---

## 6. Acceptable Reference Sources

The following sources are generally acceptable:

* Peer-reviewed journals (Scopus, Web of Science indexed)
* Conference proceedings
* Recognized standards (ASTM, ISO, BIS)
* Academic textbooks
* Government or institutional reports
* Open-access datasets (Dataverse, OpenML, etc.)

---

## 7. Restricted and Prohibited Sources

Submissions must NOT include:

* proprietary or confidential documents
* copyrighted figures or content without permission
* private datasets without access rights
* sensitive or restricted information
* unverifiable personal communications

---

## 8. Domain Scope Policy

### 8.1 Accepted Domains (STEM Only)

The repository currently accepts submissions from the following domains:

* Materials Science and Engineering
* Mechanical Engineering
* Manufacturing and Industrial Engineering
* Aerospace Engineering
* Chemical and Process Engineering
* Electrical and Electronics Engineering
* Civil and Structural Engineering
* Environmental Engineering
* Artificial Intelligence applied to STEM
* Computational Science
* Applied Mathematics
* Physics and related sciences

---

### 8.2 Encouraged Topics

Submissions are particularly encouraged if they address:

* sustainability and resource efficiency
* materials performance and reliability
* engineering optimization
* computational modeling and simulation
* data-driven scientific analysis

---

### 8.3 Excluded Domains

The following are currently out of scope:

* Social sciences
* Humanities
* Business and management
* Finance and economics
* Political science
* General policy analysis

**Reason:**
These domains require different ethical frameworks and validation mechanisms not yet supported by this repository.

---

## 9. Boundary Cases

Submissions that lie at the intersection of domains (e.g., AI in healthcare or environmental policy) will be accepted only if:

* the core contribution is technical or scientific,
* the methodology is grounded in STEM principles, and
* no sensitive or ethical violations are present.

---

## 10. Data Usage and Citation Policy

When using external datasets:

* Proper citation is mandatory
* Licensing conditions must be respected
* Public/open datasets are preferred
* Authors' permissions must be followed where applicable

---

## 11. Compliance Checklist

Before submission, ensure:

* [ ] References categorized correctly
* [ ] APA formatting applied
* [ ] AI-generated references preserved
* [ ] Verification status clearly marked
* [ ] No restricted data included
* [ ] Domain falls within STEM scope

---

## 12. Policy Intent Statement

This policy is designed not to restrict contributions, but to ensure that all submissions remain:

* scientifically traceable,
* ethically responsible, and
* useful for analyzing AI-generated scientific reasoning.

By enforcing structured reference handling and domain boundaries, the repository aims to build a reliable and reusable dataset for the research community.
