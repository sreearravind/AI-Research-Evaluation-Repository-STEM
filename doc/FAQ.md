# FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

This document provides guidance for contributors submitting AI-generated scientific reports to the repository.

---

## 1. General Questions

### Q1. What is the purpose of this repository?

This repository serves as an open scientific archive for AI-generated research reports. It enables researchers to study AI reasoning, reliability, and limitations in STEM domains.

---

### Q2. Is this a journal?

No. This is **not a peer-reviewed journal**.

It is an **open research dataset and repository** designed for:

* studying AI-generated scientific content
* benchmarking AI models
* analyzing hallucinations and reasoning patterns

---

### Q3. Who can submit?

The repository accepts submissions from:

* Students
* Academic researchers
* Industry professionals
* Independent contributors interested in STEM research

---

## 2. Submission Process

### Q4. Do I need to follow the submission template strictly?

Yes. The template ensures:

* consistency
* reproducibility
* structured evaluation of AI outputs

Submissions that do not follow the template may be rejected.

---

### Q5. Can I modify the AI-generated output?

No.

* AI outputs must be included **exactly as generated**.
* Any analysis or correction should be placed in the **Analysis section**, not in the output itself.

---

### Q6. What type of research topics are allowed?

Only **STEM-related topics** are accepted, including:

* Engineering (Mechanical, Materials, Electrical, etc.)
* Physical sciences
* Computational science
* AI applied to scientific problems

---

### Q7. Are negative or incorrect AI results allowed?

Yes.

In fact, they are highly valuable for:

* understanding AI limitations
* hallucination analysis
* improving future models

---

## 3. AI and Prompt Documentation

### Q8. Why is prompt disclosure mandatory?

Prompt disclosure ensures:

* reproducibility
* transparency
* fair benchmarking of AI models

---

### Q9. What if I used multiple prompts?

All prompts must be documented in sequence, including:

* initial prompts
* refinement prompts
* system prompts (if applicable)

---

### Q10. What AI models can be used?

Any AI model can be used, including:

* OpenAI models
* open-source LLMs
* domain-specific AI tools

However, full model metadata must be provided.

---

## 4. References and Data

### Q11. Can I use datasets from public sources?

Yes, provided that:

* proper citation is given
* licensing conditions are followed

---

### Q12. Do I need permission to use published data?

For open-access datasets, citation is usually sufficient.

For restricted or proprietary data, explicit permission is required.

---

### Q13. What should I do if the AI generates fake references?

Do not remove them.

* Mark them as **hallucinated**
* Include them in the references section

This is important for AI evaluation studies.

---

## 5. Ethics and Compliance

### Q14. Can I submit confidential or industrial data?

No.

Submissions must NOT include:

* proprietary datasets
* confidential industrial information
* restricted documents

---

### Q15. Is AI-generated content considered valid scientific evidence?

No.

All submissions must clearly state that:

* the content is AI-generated
* it is not peer-reviewed
* independent validation is required

---

## 6. Repository Usage

### Q16. How can researchers use this repository?

Researchers can use this repository to:

* benchmark AI models
* study hallucination patterns
* analyze AI reasoning in STEM
* develop improved AI systems

---

### Q17. Can I reuse submissions from this repository?

Yes, subject to:

* repository license
* proper attribution

---

## 7. Practical Guidance

### Q18. What makes a good submission?

A high-quality submission includes:

* a clear hypothesis
* well-structured prompts
* transparent methodology
* honest analysis of AI performance

---

### Q19. Should I aim for perfect results?

No.

The goal is not perfection, but:

* transparency
* reproducibility
* honest reporting

---

### Q20. Where should I start as a beginner?

Start with:

1. A simple STEM problem
2. A clear prompt
3. A single AI model
4. Basic validation of results

---

## 8. Future Scope

### Q21. Will non-STEM domains be included in the future?

Possibly.

Expansion to other domains will depend on:

* ethical frameworks
* validation mechanisms
* governance maturity of the repository

---

* transparency over perfection

Your contribution helps advance the understanding of AI in scientific research.
