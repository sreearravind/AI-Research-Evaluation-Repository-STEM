# PURPOSE AND SCOPE

## 1. Purpose of the Repository

This repository is an open, transparent, and structured research archive for the submission, documentation, and study of **AI-generated scientific reports** in **STEM and related technical domains**.

Its primary purpose is **not** to function as a conventional scientific journal, nor to validate scientific truth claims in the traditional peer-reviewed sense. Instead, the repository serves as a **systematic evaluation environment** for understanding how artificial intelligence systems generate scientific and technical content, where they perform well, where they fail, and how such outputs should be interpreted responsibly.

The repository is intended to support the research community by creating a public evidence base for studying:

- the competence of AI models in domain-specific scientific writing,
- hallucination patterns and technical failure modes,
- the reliability of AI-assisted scientific generation workflows,
- reproducibility of prompt-based scientific outputs, and
- responsible human–AI collaboration in technical knowledge production.

In this framework, the **AI-generated report is the artifact under study**. The repository therefore preserves not only the generated report itself, but also the surrounding generation context, including prompts, model metadata, verification notes, references, and contributor roles.

---

## 2. Core Mission

The mission of this repository is to build an openly accessible scientific infrastructure that helps researchers, educators, engineers, and AI developers examine the capabilities and limitations of AI-generated research outputs in a transparent and reproducible way.

The repository exists to advance the following goals:

### 2.1 Evaluate AI Model Competence in Technical Domains
The repository provides structured submissions that allow the research community to examine how well AI models perform in narrow, knowledge-intensive scientific contexts. General-purpose AI benchmarks do not adequately capture the complexities of materials science, engineering, computational science, environmental systems, and other technical fields. This repository contributes toward that gap by enabling domain-grounded evaluation.

### 2.2 Improve Transparency in AI-Assisted Research
Scientific and technical communities are increasingly using AI systems in drafting, summarization, ideation, and analytical assistance. However, such use is often insufficiently documented. This repository promotes transparent disclosure of AI involvement by requiring clear documentation of the model provider, model version, generation context, prompt structure, and date of generation.

### 2.3 Support Reproducible Human–AI Research Workflows
The repository encourages reproducibility by preserving the structured inputs that shaped the generated report. This helps future researchers study how prompt design, model configuration, and contributor decisions influence the quality and limitations of AI-generated scientific outputs.

### 2.4 Build an Open Evidence Base for Research Integrity and Safety
The repository is also intended to support broader research on AI reliability, safety, and responsible deployment in STEM workflows. By archiving both successful and flawed outputs, the repository helps identify where expert verification is essential and where AI-generated content may be inappropriate for direct scientific or engineering use.

### 2.5 Promote Scientific Utility for Human Benefit
The repository encourages submissions that address technically meaningful research questions connected to sustainability, engineering efficiency, scientific understanding, and practical benefit to society. This is not a platform for speculative content generation; it is a structured archive for studying the usefulness and limitations of AI in contributing to scientific and technical problem framing.

---

## 3. What This Repository Is

This repository is:

- an **open archive** of AI-generated scientific and technical reports;
- a **research dataset** for studying AI model behavior in STEM contexts;
- a **documentation framework** for transparent AI-assisted report generation;
- a **benchmarking resource** for comparative model evaluation across technical tasks;
- a **governed contribution platform** that records contributor roles, prompts, generation metadata, and verification notes; and
- a **public scientific resource** for studying the interaction between human intent and AI-generated technical reasoning.

The repository is designed to help the community answer questions such as:

- How reliably can AI systems generate structured scientific reports in specialized technical domains?
- What kinds of hallucinations or citation errors appear in AI-generated STEM outputs?
- Which scientific tasks appear more compatible with AI assistance, and which remain unsafe without strong human verification?
- How do prompts, generation settings, and model choice affect scientific output quality?

---

## 4. What This Repository Is Not

To avoid misuse or misunderstanding, the repository explicitly states that it is **not** any of the following:

### 4.1 Not a Conventional Peer-Reviewed Journal
This repository does not claim that accepted submissions are scientifically validated, experimentally proven, or peer-reviewed in the same sense as formal journal publications.

### 4.2 Not a Platform for Establishing Scientific Facts
Reports archived here must not be treated as authoritative sources for material properties, engineering design values, scientific laws, medical or policy recommendations, or any other form of validated fact.

### 4.3 Not a Mechanism for Challenging Existing Literature
The repository does not exist to refute, replace, or overrule established scientific literature. If an AI-generated report contradicts established knowledge, that contradiction is treated as a point of analysis about model behavior, not as evidence against the literature.

### 4.4 Not a Hidden AI Writing Service
This repository does not support undisclosed or deceptive AI use. Its purpose is the opposite: explicit and auditable disclosure of AI involvement in report generation.

### 4.5 Not a Repository for Sensitive or Restricted Research Material
The repository must not be used to archive confidential, proprietary, restricted, classified, or ethically sensitive materials. Open and non-sensitive scientific documentation is required.

## 5. Scope of Accepted Contributions

At the present stage, the repository accepts submissions only from **STEM and closely related technical research areas**.

### 5.1 Included Domains
Examples of in-scope domains include, but are not limited to:

- materials science and engineering,
- metallurgy,
- mechanical engineering,
- manufacturing and industrial engineering,
- aerospace engineering,
- chemical and process engineering,
- civil and structural engineering,
- electrical and electronics engineering,
- environmental engineering,
- computational science,
- artificial intelligence applied to STEM problems,
- applied mathematics,
- scientific computing,
- physics and related physical sciences, and
- technically grounded sustainability research within STEM contexts.

### 5.2 Excluded Domains
At this time, the repository does **not** accept primary submissions in:

- social sciences,
- humanities,
- business and management,
- political science,
- general public policy,
- finance and economics, and
- other domains where the current repository governance and evaluation framework are not yet appropriate.

This restriction exists because those fields require different validation frameworks, ethical safeguards, interpretive standards, and domain review practices. The present repository is intentionally focused on technical and scientific domains where factual, methodological, and referential assessment can be more systematically structured.

---

## 6. Why the Repository Is Open and Decentralized

The repository is designed as an open and decentralized submission environment because the study of AI-generated scientific reporting should not be limited to a small institutional gatekeeping structure.

Open contribution supports:

- broader participation from students, researchers, industry professionals, and independent technical contributors;
- diverse prompt strategies and scientific problem framings;
- comparative study across domains, model providers, and contributor backgrounds;
- more realistic evidence of how AI is actually being used in scientific contexts; and
- a richer archive for understanding human–AI interaction in STEM research generation.

This openness does not mean lack of rigor. Rather, it means that rigor is achieved through **transparent documentation, submission standards, metadata requirements, verification practices, and repository governance**, instead of relying solely on closed editorial control.

## 7. Intended Contributors

This repository is open to contributors who wish to responsibly document and submit AI-generated scientific reports in line with repository standards.

Contributors may include:

- students,
- academic researchers,
- postdoctoral researchers,
- faculty members,
- industrial researchers,
- STEM professionals,
- technical practitioners, and
- independent contributors working in or closely connected to STEM fields.

Contributors are expected to respect the repository’s transparency and integrity requirements, including proper documentation of AI involvement, references, contributor roles, and generation context.

---

## 8. Scientific and Ethical Position

The repository is built on the principle that responsible AI use in science requires **disclosure, caution, traceability, and governance**.

Accordingly, every accepted submission should make clear that:

- the report is AI-generated or AI-assisted,
- the submission is **not a peer-reviewed scientific publication**,
- the output may contain hallucinations, inaccuracies, omissions, or fabricated references,
- independent verification is required before relying on the content for research, engineering, educational, or policy use, and
- the purpose of archiving the report is to study AI-generated scientific reasoning and documentation practice.

The repository strongly discourages the use of:

- proprietary or confidential datasets without permission,
- copyrighted journal figures or restricted illustrations,
- sensitive personal, medical, defense, or legally restricted information, and
- misleading presentation of AI-generated content as fully human-authored work.

---

## 9. Transparency Requirements

A central purpose of this repository is to ensure that AI involvement in scientific report generation is documented clearly and consistently.

For this reason, submissions should capture, at minimum:

- AI model provider,
- model name and version,
- generation date,
- prompt architect or research initiator,
- prompt text or generation context,
- AI-generated tables, figures, and references,
- any supplied input materials or source references,
- verification notes on citations or technical claims where applicable, and
- contributor role attribution.

The repository values transparency not as an administrative burden, but as the basis for reproducibility, contributor credit, and responsible downstream analysis.

---

## 10. Contribution Value for the Research Community

The value of this repository lies not only in storing outputs, but in enabling the research community to study patterns across many submissions.

Over time, the repository can support research on:

- model-specific hallucination behavior,
- citation fabrication patterns,
- technical reasoning strengths and weaknesses,
- reproducibility of prompt-based scientific generation,
- task-level safety and applicability of AI in STEM workflows,
- comparative benchmarking across model families, and
- human–AI collaboration practices in scientific documentation.

The repository may therefore function as a foundational dataset for future scholarly work on AI-generated scientific reasoning, technical communication, and open evaluation infrastructures.

---

## 11. Repository-Level Statement on Scientific Integrity

This repository is founded on scientific integrity through transparency rather than through claims of authority.

Accordingly:

- submissions are archived as documented AI-generated artifacts;
- contributor roles are recorded to protect conceptual credit;
- AI involvement must be explicitly disclosed;
- technical claims should be treated cautiously and verified independently;
- references must be documented and clearly distinguished between input sources and AI-generated references where applicable; and
- the repository’s role is to support analysis of AI-generated scientific reports, not to certify their scientific correctness.

---

## 12. Future Evolution of Scope

The repository begins with a STEM-focused scope because this is the most appropriate and manageable starting point for rigorous technical evaluation.

Future expansion into adjacent or more sensitive domains may be considered only after the repository develops:

- stronger governance maturity,
- domain-specific review capacity,
- clearer evaluation protocols for non-STEM fields, and
- adequate ethical safeguards for broader subject areas.

Until then, the repository remains intentionally focused on technical and scientific domains.

---

## 13. Summary Statement

In summary, this repository is an open scientific infrastructure for the transparent submission and study of AI-generated reports in STEM and related technical fields.

It exists to document how AI systems generate scientific content, how contributors interact with these systems, and how such outputs should be evaluated, interpreted, and governed responsibly.

It is not a journal of validated scientific truth. It is a structured archive for studying AI-generated scientific reporting itself.

By maintaining openness, traceability, contributor credit, and ethical documentation standards, the repository aims to support the research community in building a deeper and more evidence-based understanding of AI’s role in future scientific work.
